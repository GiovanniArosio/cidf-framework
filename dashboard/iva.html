Component 02 · Communicative Layer

IVAInterpretive Void Analyzer — reported individually

A set of NLP instruments measuring communicative disruption. The four dimensions are reported individually, not as a single forced composite: two are CORE inputs to the Interpretive Vector, one is CONTEXTUAL, and one is a DIAGNOSTIC excluded from every aggregate. All outputs are exploratory and corpus-bound.

01

Four dimensions, by case

SMALL MULTIPLES

Attribution Drift and Narrative Fragmentation are CORE (each weighted 0.5 in the Interpretive Vector). Response Timing is CONTEXTUAL — and unavailable for KA-SAT, where early-window evidence is insufficient and is never imputed. Technical–Public Gap is a DIAGNOSTIC, shown here as cosine distance but excluded from every CIDI formula.

02

Attribution Drift — internals

WEIGHTED 4-COMPONENT MODEL
02.1

Sub-component breakdown

Drift = weighted mean of actor plurality (0.30), temporal instability (0.25), convergence delay (0.25) and confidence dispersion (0.20). All three cases identify a single actor (Russia), so actor-plurality is 0 throughout — drift is driven by timing and confidence, not by competing culprits.
02.2

IVC core composition

The Interpretive Vector combines the two CORE dimensions with equal weight: IVC = 0.5 × Attribution Drift + 0.5 × Narrative Fragmentation. Fragmentation dominates the vector in all three cases.
03

Attribution timelines

SEQUENCE → CONVERGENCE

Each dot is an attribution-related document positioned by date, from the crisis date to the last sampled document. Convergence requires three consecutive documents naming the same specific actor. KA-SAT opens with two unresolved claims before converging on Russia after 59 days; NotPetya converges quickly but takes 233 days from crisis to its first in-scope attribution; PAP converges in a single day amid a compressed, days-long sequence.

04

Narrative Fragmentation

K-MEANS · k = 3 / 4 / 5 SENSITIVITY
04.1

Cluster-count sensitivity

Diversity of competing narratives via K-Means on sentence embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2). Reported across k = 3 / 4 / 5; k = 4 is primary (highlighted). Scores rise monotonically with k, as expected — the ordering between cases is stable.
04.2

Dimension roles

What the numbers say Interpretive disruption does not track technical complexity. PAP — the least technically complex case — carries the second-highest IVC (0.353), just above KA-SAT (0.342), because its narrative fragmentation is high despite a fast, unanimous attribution. The "interpretive void" is widest where a low-sophistication information operation still generates competing narratives in the public sphere — precisely the asymmetry the CIDI weighting is designed to surface.
