Component 01 · Technical Layer

TCITechnical Complexity Index — evidence-aware

Operational sophistication scored on the MITRE ATT&CK framework across five components. The model is evidence-aware: each component carries a status, and unknown evidence is never treated as documented absence. Outputs distinguish a conservative floor, an evidence-adjusted score, an assessed score including inference, and the underlying evidence coverage.

02

Evidence-status matrix

CASES × ATT&CK COMPONENTS

Each cell shows the component's raw value and its evidence status. The evidence-adjusted score is the mean of documented components only — inferred values inform the floor but not the adjusted score, and unknown components are excluded entirely rather than scored as zero. PAP's low score is driven by missing public technical evidence (three unknowns), not by a documented absence of capability.

03

Score variants & coverage

FLOOR · EVIDENCE-ADJ · ASSESSED
03.1

Three transparent score variants

Floor = conservative lower bound (inferred at floor, unknown = 0). Evidence-adjusted = documented components only (primary). Assessed = includes inference in the mean.
03.2

Evidence coverage

Share of applicable components with documented evidence. Shown alongside scores but never folded into them.
Reading the gradient TCIEA falls monotonically from NotPetya (0.662) → KA-SAT (0.550) → PAP (0.250), but so does evidence coverage (0.80 → 0.60 → 0.40). The framework keeps these two quantities separate: PAP's score is low because most of its kill-chain is undocumented in the public corpus, which is a statement about available evidence, not a claim that the operation was technically trivial. This caveat is what the evidence-aware design exists to preserve.
